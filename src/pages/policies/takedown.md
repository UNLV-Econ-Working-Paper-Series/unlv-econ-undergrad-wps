The Series is an educational archive. Rights, privacy, and policy concerns are reviewed seriously.

## Reasons we may restrict access or remove content

We may restrict access, remove a PDF, or withdraw a paper page for reasons including:

- credible copyright claims or unauthorized third-party content
- privacy concerns (for example, personal data that should not be public)
- defamation or unlawful-content allegations
- proven research misconduct or serious policy violations
- administrative or legal requirements

## How to submit a request

Send an email to the series team listed on the [Contact page](../../contact/) with:

- paper title and URL
- specific content at issue (page numbers, figure, excerpt)
- basis for the request (copyright, privacy, etc.)
- supporting documentation or proof of rights/identity when applicable
- preferred resolution (remove PDF, redact content, correct metadata, and so on)

## What happens next

The editorial team typically:

1. acknowledges receipt
2. reviews the request and supporting materials
3. contacts the author(s) when appropriate
4. decides on a response (no change, correction, restricted access, or withdrawal)

Temporary restriction may occur while review is ongoing.

## Possible outcomes

- metadata correction
- PDF replacement (revised version)
- PDF removal while leaving a public tombstone page for citation continuity
- full withdrawal when necessary

## Appeals

If a requester or author disagrees with the outcome, the matter may be escalated through the appropriate academic administrative channel when the Series is operated by UNLV.
