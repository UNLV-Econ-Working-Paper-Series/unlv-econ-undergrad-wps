# Writing Standards

These standards apply to public-facing copy for the UNLV Undergraduate Economics Working Paper Series. They also guide repository documentation when it describes the site, publication workflow, policies, or governance.

## Core Rules

- Use plain institutional language.
- Prefer concrete nouns and verbs.
- Use "working paper series" or "archive" as the default description.
- Do not describe working papers as peer-reviewed unless explicitly true.
- Do not imply official institutional approval beyond what the series governance supports.
- Make disclaimers direct and visible.
- Public pages should sound like a university research archive, not marketing copy.

## Avoid

Avoid inflated phrases such as:

- "seamless"
- "robust"
- "cutting-edge"
- "transformative"
- "delve"
- "tapestry"
- "dynamic"
- "leverage"
- "empower"
- "unlock"
- "elevate"
- "foster innovation"

Also avoid similar filler, generic overviews that say little, unsupported claims about excellence, impact, prestige, innovation, or leadership, and excessive adjectives.

## Sentence Style

- Avoid excessive parallel sentence structures.
- Avoid repetitive "not only... but also..." framing.
- Replace "designed to" with a direct verb when the meaning is the same.
- Keep sentences clear enough for students, faculty, administrators, and outside readers.

## Use Specific Facts

When writing about papers or issues, include specific facts where available:

- issue
- semester
- paper title
- author
- category
- PDF
- citation
- correction or versioning policy

## Policy Pages

Policy pages should be clear, factual, and operational. State who the page is for, what the policy covers, what action a reader can take, and what information the series needs from them.

Do not use policy pages to make broad claims about the series' quality, reach, or institutional importance.

## Deployment Terms

Use "Faculty Sites" and "cPanel" only in repository and deployment documentation. Avoid those terms in public-facing reader copy unless a reader needs them to complete a task.

## Before and After

Bad:

> This dynamic platform empowers students to showcase innovative economic research.

Better:

> This site publishes undergraduate economics working papers from the UNLV Department of Economics.
